To Remember to Remember: Reflection on the Literary Memoirs of Filipino Women

Cristina Pantoja Hidalgo

PHP 400.00

This book picks up a story which the author began to tell in 1994, with autobiographical narratives of Filipino women, Filipino Woman Writing (Second Edition, UST Publishing House, 2015). Here, Cristina Pantoja Hidalgo turns to the first women writers to own the name “literary memoirist.” Four generations of women writers are represented in this unusual volume: Paz Policarpio-Mendez; Solita Camara-Besa; Gilda Cordero-Fernando; Merlie Alunan; Criselda Yabes; Jenny Ortuoste; and Rica Bolipata-Santos. Hidalgo shows how these writers have crafted stories from memories that continue to haunt them, using language whose shapeliness transform them into compelling experiences for their readers.

Cristina Pantoja Hidalgo is a writer of fiction and nonfiction, a critic and a literary scholar, with more than 40 published books. As an academic, she pioneered in the writing and teaching of Creative Nonfiction in the country. A Book of Dreams is her third novel. The other two are Vida (Mr. & Ms, 1981), and Recuerdo (UP Press, 1996, 2020). She has received multiple literary awards, the latest being the S.E.A.Write Award, an international award granted by the Royal Family of Thailand. She has also received national awards for lifetime achievement: the Carlos Palanca Dangal ng Lahi Award, the Gawad Balagtas Award from the Unyon ng mga Manunulat sa Pilipinas (UMPIL), and the Parangal Hagbong from the UST Varsitarian; and various Palancas and National Book Awards for individual works. She has worked as a teacher, writer, and editor in Bangkok, Seoul, Yangon, New York City, and Manila. As an academic administrator, she has served in the University of the Philippines as Vice President for Public Affairs of the UP System, Director of the UP Press, and Director of the UP Institute of Creative Center; and in UST as Director of the UST Publishing House. At present, she is the Director of the UST Center for Creative Writing and Literary Studies, and Professor Emeritus of UP Diliman.
