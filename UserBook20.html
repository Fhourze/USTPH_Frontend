Looking For Polaris: A Memoir of Losing and Finding

Dawn Laurente Marfil

PHP 300.00

Candidly poetic, Dawn Laurente-Marfil vividly paints the people in her life in her essays on self, love, loss and moving forward in her debut autobiographical collection. From her growing up years, romantic relationships, heartbreaks, and musings to her early beginnings as a writer, Marfil’s narratives tantalizingly mirror the lives of Filipinas of all ages at different points in life.

Dawn Marie Nicole L. Marfil graduated cum laude from University of Santo Tomas (UST) with a Bachelor of Arts degree in Literature. She then earned her Master of Arts degree in Creative Writing from the University of the Philippines, Diliman. She was a fellow at the 3rd J. Elizalde Navarro National Workshop for Arts Criticism, 2011, and at the UST Creative Writing Workshop, 2012. Her nonfiction piece, “Roche’s Limit,” was previously published in Tomas, Volume 2, Issue 1, of the 1st semester 2012–2013, while a small collection of her poems was published in the Far Eastern University English and Literature Journal, Volume 6, No.1, in 2012. She teaches Literature and Humanities subjects in UST and whenever she can, she hibernates. She lives alone in a condo on the border between Quezon City and Manila where she tries DIY projects, recipes, hairstyling techniques, and just about anything she can find on Pinterest.
