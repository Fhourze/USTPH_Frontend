Fiction-Novel

Lifeboat

Kristine Ong Muslim

PHP 300.00

This haunting collection solidifies Muslim’s reputation as a poet of loss, of absence. The often surreal, always sharp poems in the collection accumulate into a haunting whole. The speaker invites readers to enter one of the most brilliant poetic minds writing today. Muslim finds beauty in the most unlikely of places and create a world unimagined since the time of the surrealists.

Kristine Ong Muslim is the author of We Bury the Landscape (Texas: Queen’s Ferry Press, 2012), Grim Series (Wisconsin: Popcorn Press, 2012), and A Roomful of Machines (New York: ELJ Publications, 2015), a new edition of her debut poetry collection that came out in 2012. Her forthcoming books include the short story collection, Age of Blight (Los Angeles: Unnamed Press, 2016) and the poetry collection, Black Arcadia (Quezon City: University of the Philippines Press, 2016). Her poems and short stories have been widely anthologized and published in magazines such as the Boston Review, Confrontation Magazine, Existere, and New Welsh Review. She lives in southern Philippines and serves as poetry editor of LONTAR: The Journal of Southeast Asian Speculative Fiction, a literary journal published by Epigram Books in Singapore. Lifeboat is her fourth book.
