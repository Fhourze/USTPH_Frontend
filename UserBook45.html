Loose Tongue: Poems 2001-2013

Carlomar Arcangel Daoana

PHP 300.00

In this collection, which gathers poems from twelve years of the author’s lyric engagements outside of his three previous poetry books, including a handful of works that haven’t been collected, Daoana intones the world, both with the ease with which youthful lyricism professes knowledge of it and with the philosophical heft that acknowledges its limits. The poems are brazen in their admission of breakage, suppliant in their appraisal of the damage. This book is the incontrovertible proof that faith springs from every worlding’s keen sense of the end.

Carlomar Arcangel Daoana is the author of Marginal Bliss (UP Press, 2002) and The Fashionista’s Book of Enlightenment (Designed By Words, 2009), finalists in the National Book Awards, as well as Clairvoyance (UST Publishing House, 2011). He has won the Carlos Palanca Memorial Awards for Literature in the English Poetry category for his collections, “The Elegant Ghost” (grand prize) and “Crown for Maria” (second prize) in 2012 and 2013, respectively. A graduate of the University of Santo Tomas with a degree in Literature, he served as a junior associate for poetry at the UST Center for Creative Writing and Literary Studies. Currently, he contributes lifestyle and arts and culture articles to various print and online publications and is finishing his MFA Creative Writing degree at De La Salle University.
