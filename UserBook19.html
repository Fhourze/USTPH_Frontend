Home Of the Ashfall:A Memoir

John Jack G. Wigley

PHP 200.00

Yet wrestling with hard-pressing issues, Wigley remains a funny storyteller in his memoir Home of the Ashfall: A Memoir. Filled with narrative whiplash, his essays recount his mother’s life, and his grief over her passing, and while it appears to be a maternal tribute, the book is also very much about his search for a father. Funny, heartbreaking, and enlightening, his story, in his telling, triggers one’s own recollection, mirroring their experience until it slowly becomes their own.

John Jack G. Wigley is the author of Falling into the Manhole (2012) which was a finalist in the Madrigal-Gonzalez Best First Book Award, Home of the Ashfall (2014), Lait Chronicles (2016), which was a finalist in the National Book Awards, Lait (pa more) Chronicles (2017), Hantong: Stories (2018), and Kadenang Bahaghari (2019). He is also the translator of today’s featured book, Pag-Ibig sa Panahon ng Kolera (2022) by Gabriel Garcia Marquez. Currently, Dr. Wigley is a professor at the University of Santo Tomas (UST).
